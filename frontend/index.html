How it works

Pixel Mosaic takes two images — a Source and a Target — and rebuilds the Target's shape using only the pixels that belong to the Source. No new colours are invented and no blending happens: every pixel in the output is a real Source pixel, repositioned to form the Target's silhouette. The Source is the palette of tiles; the Target is the blueprint that decides where each tile goes.

The result is a five-second WebGL animation in which every pixel flies from its original position in the Source to its mapped position on the Target, plus a downloadable PNG of the final frame. The Target's structure is preserved with pixel-perfect fidelity, while the Source's spatial arrangement is scrambled away — only its colour palette survives. Point a forest at a portrait and the portrait comes back made of foliage.
